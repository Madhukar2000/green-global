Operations


MANUFACTURING:

Global Green Company has four world-class manufacturing facilities – two In India and two in Hungary. These include locations in Bangalore (Oblapura), Hyderabad (Venkatapur), Dunakiliti and Balmazújváros (Puszta).

All Global Green Company’s facilities are strategically located within six hours driving distance of their agri operations and have all the requisite quality certifications for the markets and customers they serve. A core engineering team works continuously on implementing the latest innovations in manufacturing technologies across Global Green Company’s facilities.

Venkatapur, Hyderabad, India

The Venkatapur plant was set up in 1996 across an area of 54.23 hectares. The facility has a total built-up area of 14,500 square metres with close to 20 hectares of plant area. The plant has a multi-product packing operation for gherkins (wholes & cut), jalapeños, relish and tomato based products, with capability for fresh pack and processed pack in retail packs (glass jars and PET), foodservice packs (pouches and tins) and industrial packs (drums and pails). With a capacity of preserving more than 1,500 metric tons of gherkins in underground brining tanks, this plant specializes in packing relish and other de-brined products.

Oblapura, Bangalore, India

The Oblapura plant was set up in 2004 across an area of 8.28 hectares with a total built-up area of 11,989 square meters. The plant is located in the city of Bangalore and has jar, canning and PET lines.

The plant has a multi-product packing operation for gherkins (wholes & cut), jalapeños and baby corn, with capability for fresh pack in retail packs (glass jars and PET), foodservice packs (tins) and industrial packs (drums and pails). Though Oblapura packs all gherkin grades, it focuses and specializes in packing smaller grades – cornichons and petites.

Dunakiliti, North Western Hungary

The Dunakiliti plant was set up in 1991 across an area of 7 hectares. The facility has a total built-up area of 18,000 square meters. The plant has a multi-product packing operation for cherries, gherkins (wholes & cut), silverskin onions, pritamin paprika and capers products, with capability for fresh pack and processed pack in retail packs (glass jars and PET), and industrial packs (cans).

Puszta, Eastern Hungary

Located in the heart of corn and pea plantations, the Puszta plant was set up in 1994 across an area of 8 hectares. The facility has a total built up area of 15,000 square meters. The plant has a multi-product packing operation for green peas, corn (sweet & super sweet), tomato and paprika products, with capability for fresh pack in retail packs (glass jars and cans).

QUALITY AND FOOD SAFETY:

Safety and quality of the product is enabled “by design” right at the product development stage by installing appropriate machinery, ensuring due diligence, establishing and practicing optimized processes at all levels of the value chain. These are validated periodically to ensure that the methods opted help deliver consistent results.

To ensure safety of the agri-based commodities, an integrated residue monitoring protocol has been established and extensive statistical sampling is carried out at farms as well as upon receipt at manufacturing sites. Farmers and field teams are trained on food safety and quality requirements at regular intervals to deliver safe produce.

A risk based vendor approval process has been established for sourcing high quality input materials. Consistency in quality of every input is measured and monitored by inspecting consignments upon arrival at manufacturing sites.

Effectiveness of systems and processes have been established and are implemented by our partners and reviewed onsite periodically. Sustainability of the established food safety and integrated quality management system are verified through internal and external certification audits.

Specific quality and food safety objectives are set for every function, which are reviewed periodically by top management as a

part of a well-established performance management system. Customer feedback is obtained on the performance of groups and objectives are reviewed and aligned to customer requirements

Good Agricultural Practices (GAP) at farms are implemented, current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) at all manufacturing locations, and effective logistics across the value chain enhance our commitment towards a common goal.

A risk-based approach aligned to the Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) is adopted for all relevant processes to ensure that there is no compromise on food safety, food security and quality of our products. Effectiveness of the system is validated through periodic reviews and challenge tests wherever appropriate. US FDA Inspected PLC based thermal processes are in place and schedule process compliance is monitored for every lot packed in our facilities.

Preventive controls are established to avoid possible intrusion of foreign matter into products and the lines are further strengthened with advanced metal detection and X-ray scanning systems. Product testing is carried out at well-equipped in house laboratories and at ISO 17025 accredited laboratories in consistent fashion to assure that the product delivered is safe for consumption.

To comply with regulatory requirements for each country of destination, an exclusive regulatory compliance division has been established that looks after updating new regulations and evaluating compliance across the system. Quality and food safety strategies are derived as a part of our Annual Operating Plan (AOP) and food safety initiatives for continual improvement are determined based upon the onsite risk assessment by cross functional teams
